The Biosphere and Short Term Carbon Cycle:

1. What is the biosphere?

  • The biosphere is crucial for cycling nutrients and carbon throughout the Earth's system
  • The biosphere supports life in the ocean, atmosphere, land, surface, and soil
  • The biosphere also plays an important dynamic role in Earth's system

2. What are autotrophs?

  • Autotrophs are primary producers
  • Autotrophs are photosynthesizers and there are chemosynthesizers
  • Energy needed from life comes from autotrophs

3. Explain Photosynthesis

  • Autotrophs take in sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water
  • They use this to produce oxygen and glucose
  • Energy needed from life comes from autotrophs

4. What is a biogeochemical cycle?

  • Biogeochemical cycles are tracking a single element throughout the biosphere and other earth system spheres

5. Explain the difference between organic and inorganic carbon

  • Organic carbon contain c-c and c-h bonds and inorganic carbon does not contain them.
  • Organic carbon can be formed by living organisms while inorganic carbon typically isn't formed by lving organisms
  • Organic carbon examples are sugars and carbs, and inorganic carbon examples are carbonates and carbon dioxide

6. Explain the difference between oxidized and reduced carbon

  • Oxidized carbon forms with oxygen while reduced carbon forms with C,N,H
  • Oxidized carbon is inorganic [c. dioxide and carbonates] are more inert and reduced carbon is organic and reactive

7. What are reservoirs? Which reservoirs have fast fluxes, and which have slow fluxes?

  • Reservoirs are characterized by the amount of material they are holding at a particular time
  • Fast: atmospheric methane, living biomass, atmospheric carbon dioxide, oceanic dissolved carbon dioxide
  • Slow: Oceanic bicarbonate ion, organic carbon in sedementary rocks, limestone in sedementary rocks

8. How do reservoirs become inbalanced?

  • Reservoirs become inbalanced do to perturbations in the system
  • This is important to remember for the smaller reservoirs with fast fluxes

9. What is steady state and how is it maitained?

  • Steady state is when the level in the reservoir is constant with time
  • For this to be maintained, the inflow/outflow rates must be sensitive to changes in the reservoir size

10. What is residence time?

  • Residence time is the average length of time some substance spends in a given reservoir @ steady state
  • Residence_Time = Steady_State_Reservoir.size() / Inflow_Or_OutFlow_Rate

11. How is carbon transferred between the major components of the Earth's system?

  • Biosphere to atm-ocean: respiration/decay
  • Biosphere to crust: burial
  • Crust to mantle: subduction
  • Mantle to crust: sea floor spreading
  • Crust to atm-ocean: weathering
  • Mantle to atm-ocean: volcanism
